Formative Feedback


Design Evolution

The alpha system went through multiple evolutions and design changes that reflect the feedback received previously, as well as our own insights. The evolutions are as follows, in order of importance:


Evolution 1: Different Categories and Difficulties of Questions

The evaluators in our feedback reported that the questions “felt too hard for the target users”, which was students in general. In fact, they claimed that “tough object-oriented programming questions like the ones in the prototype are not suitable for students that have not studied these concepts in general”. We agree with this comment, and concluded that such a difficulty and niche of topic would decrease the usability of the system, and make it too hard to use for the average student. Furthermore, one of our main usability goals was that “the system should not be too hard to use”, and that the user should be able to accomplish the tasks under 60 seconds. To make this goal more attainable by the average student, we added difficulty levels for the questions, such as “easy/medium/hard” as well as different categories altogether, such as “spanish”, so that it’s not only bound to programming.


Evolution 2: Randomized Questions

During the Hi-fi prototype testing, our evaluators noticed that the fact that our questions would always come in the same order defeated the purpose of learning. They noticed that the users would simply remember the order of questions that had appeared previously, and memorize the answer patterns, which “defeated the purpose of the prototype”. We agree strongly with this statement. It correlates with one of our usability goals, which was that “the tasks should not be too easy to accomplish”. Indeed, if the user simply remembers the answer’s in order, the task would become too easy, and our usability goal would fail. Therefore, in order to increase usability, we randomized the order of questions in the alpha version.


Evolution 3: Minimalized System Settings

From our own insights using the system, we noticed that the settings were far too complicated to get around once you could actually locate it. in fact, in our feedback, our evaluators highlighted that the "amount of menus for so few functionalities intensified the plugin’s burden.". We agree that our settings menu was indeed very cumbersome, and this decreases the usability of the system. In fact, one of our three main usability heuristics from Nielsen's list was "Aesthetic and minimalist design". In this revision, we concluded that having three different menus for such few settings decreased the aesthetic and minimalism of the system, and would therefore reduce usability. In order to remedy this, we combined the three settings menu into a single one, where the user can make all the necessary system modifications on the same page. This reduces confusion, and makes all the functionalities of the system immediately obvious to the user, which increases usability.
